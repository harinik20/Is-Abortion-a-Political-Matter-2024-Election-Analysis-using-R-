Project Summary/Overview -->
- This study examines whether state-level abortion laws are significantly tied to presidential voting patterns in the 2024 US election.
- Using county-level voting data and state reproductive policy classifications, I conducted two Welch's 2-sample t-tests in R to evaluate differences in Democratic/Republican vote share across state abortion statuses (legal vs. illegal).
Key Findings --> 
- Democratic Vote Share: Counties in states where abortion is legal recorded a significantly higher average Democratic vote share (42.0%) than those where it is illegal.
- Republican Vote Share: Counties in states where abortion is illegal recorded a significantly higher average Republican vote share (73.4%) than those where it is legal.
Conclusion -->
- The statistical results show that abortion access and presidential voting are definetely aligned.
- State abortion bans and high Republican vote counts both stem from strong conservative control and high voter turnout following the Dobbs decision.
